Passkeys

Passkeys are a secure way to authenticate sensitive actions. Janus uses them as an extra layer of security to authenticate you before allowing you to access AWS credentials or the AWS console.

Please contact @passkeyManagerLinkText if you have any problems registering or using passkeys.

You need at least one passkey to access AWS. We recommend registering two — one on your laptop for everyday speed and convenience, and one portable passkey so that your access isn't tied to a single device.

laptop_mac On your laptop

On a Mac, register a passkey in the Chrome browser to unlock it with Touch ID.

On Windows, a fingerprint passkey may not be available, as Windows Hello is disabled by default. Use a portable passkey instead.

An on-device passkey is the fastest and most convenient option for everyday use.

A portable passkey gives you resilience and lets you sign in from other devices.
